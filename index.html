It is more than just stigma that affects those with mental illness – it is discrimination. The National Alliance on Mental Illness uses the definition of stigma as a “mark of disgrace, or a negative stereotype.” People with mental illness experience discrimination with regard to employment, education, housing, and ironically, healthcare. The mentally ill are portrayed all too often with ridicule. They are called “nuts,” and plenty of other derogatory slang words. These words are dehumanizing, and can affect the likelihood that those with mental illness will seek, stick with, or be successful in treatment. Changing the terminology with which we refer to the mentally ill is a step toward policy changes that will increase access to treatment and services for individuals and families who are suffering.
